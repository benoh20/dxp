# Gen Z Voter Outreach (18-26)

**Source:** Powerbuilder curated corpus, distilled from CIRCLE (Tufts), NextGen America, Voters of Tomorrow, and Harvard IOP Youth Poll 2018-2024.
**Date:** 2025-09-30
**Document type:** segment playbook
**Topics:** gen z, youth, students, college, registration, gotv

## What's true that is widely believed wrong

- **Gen Z does answer the door.** The under-30 turnout gap is not a "won't open the door" problem; it is a "wasn't asked" problem. When asked, Gen Z opens at roughly the same rate as the broader file.
- **Gen Z does read SMS.** 90%+ open rates within 30 minutes. They do not read email.
- **Gen Z does not pick up the phone.** Cold phone banks to under-25 voters convert at one-third the rate of any other cohort.
- **TikTok and Instagram Reels are the highest-reach digital channel** for under-25, but the conversion is brand awareness, not direct GOTV. Treat as top-of-funnel.

## Where Gen Z lives in Gwinnett

- High concentration around Georgia Gwinnett College (Lawrenceville), Gwinnett Tech (multiple campuses), and Georgia State Perimeter (Lawrenceville).
- Multifamily housing along the I-85 corridor (Norcross, Duluth, Suwanee).
- A meaningful share still live with parents — door knocks should be ready to talk to a parent first.

## What converts at the door

- A **peer canvasser** — under-30, ideally also a student or recent grad.
- An opener that names the candidate's position on **one** issue Gen Z cares about most: cost of housing, student debt, climate, gun policy, or reproductive rights. National polling shows housing cost is the #1 motivator in 2024-2026 cycles, edging out the others.
- A registration check on the spot if the voter is unsure of their status.
- A **bring-a-friend** ask. Gen Z votes in cohorts more than any other age group.

## What converts on SMS

- Short, conversational, no campaign-speak. "Hey [NAME], early vote starts Monday — your spot is [LOCATION]. Want me to send you the map?"
- An emoji is fine. A wall of emojis is not.
- A direct opt-in question early in the conversation: "Are you planning to vote early or on election day?" — sorts the universe and earns reply rate.
- Day-of-election morning text with the polling location as a tappable Google Maps link.

## What converts on social

- Short-form vertical video featuring **the candidate themselves**, not a polished spot. Authenticity > production value.
- Specific local references — naming the school, the road, the apartment complex.
- Direct CTAs to a Linktree-style landing page with: register, find your polling place, volunteer, donate.

## Common failures

- "Vote because it matters." Replace with the specific consequence of not voting (school board controls X, the rent stabilization vote is on the ballot, etc.).
- Treating Gen Z as a monolith. Gen Z men and Gen Z women polled 12+ points apart on 2024 vote choice. Different messengers, different framing.
- Asking for money before any other ask. Gen Z donor conversion is real but lags behind volunteer conversion by 3-4x.

## Conversion benchmarks

- Peer door knock + SMS follow-up to a Gen Z low-prop voter: **14-18 point turnout lift** (NextGen 2020-2022 program review).
- Same voter, no contact: ~22% turnout in midterms, ~48% in presidential.
- SMS-only with no door precedes: ~1 point lift, but valuable for plan-to-vote sorting.

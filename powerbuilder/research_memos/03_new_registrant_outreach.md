# New Registrant Outreach Playbook

**Source:** Powerbuilder curated corpus, distilled from CIRCLE (Tufts), Analyst Institute, and NextGen America program reviews 2018-2024.
**Date:** 2025-08-22
**Document type:** segment playbook
**Topics:** new registrants, voter file, gotv, youth, latinx, first-time voters

## Why new registrants need their own track

A new registrant is not a low-propensity voter — they are a no-propensity voter. The vote-history fields are blank, the partisan score is suppressed, the turnout score is suppressed. Standard targeting models will exclude them or down-weight them, which means the campaign that should be most aggressively contacting them is least likely to.

In Gwinnett-style suburban Sun Belt counties, **new registrants in the trailing 18 months are 3x more likely to be under 30, 2x more likely to be Latinx or AAPI, and 4x more likely to live in a rental unit** than the file average. Treating them as "unmodeled" rather than "low-propensity" unlocks them.

## Three things every new registrant needs to know

1. **Where to vote.** Not the address — the building name and the parking situation. Use Google Maps screenshots in mail.
2. **What to bring.** GA requires a photo ID; new registrants frequently believe their voter card is required. Tell them the card is not required; the photo ID is.
3. **What's actually on the ballot.** New registrants cite "I don't know what I'd be voting on" as the #1 reason for not voting in low-info elections. A simple plain-language ballot summary in mail or SMS converts.

## Cadence

- **First contact within 30 days of registration.** A welcome touch, no ask. "Thanks for registering — here's what's coming up."
- **60 days out from election day:** plan-to-vote ask. SMS or door, not mail (mail open rates among under-30 renters are below 20%).
- **14 days out:** location reminder.
- **GOTV weekend:** confirm time and place, day-of-election morning text.

## What works for first-time voters specifically

- **Buddy framing:** "bring a friend" pulls a meaningful share who would otherwise skip. Easy to add to any script.
- **Photo of the polling location** in SMS — first-time voters are anxious about going to the wrong place. Removing that anxiety is worth several percentage points.
- **Spanish for Spanish-surname new registrants** — the field assumes English fluency for younger Latinx voters but Spanish is still the household language for ~40% of US-born Mexican-American 18-24 year olds.

## What does not work

- Issue persuasion. New registrants are not yet partisan — they are not yet voters. Spend the contact on logistics, not on making the case for the candidate.
- Ballot drop-off encouragement in GA — the state does not have widespread drop boxes. Direct them to in-person early vote or mail return.
- "Your vote matters" abstractions. Replace with concrete: "Your vote elects the school board that runs [school name]."

## Conversion benchmarks

- New registrant + bilingual SMS plan-to-vote ask + GOTV weekend door knock: **18-24 point turnout lift** vs. no contact (Analyst Institute meta-analysis 2018-2022).
- Same voter, mail only: **3-5 point lift**.
- Same voter, no contact: baseline ~28% turnout in GA midterms, ~52% in presidential cycles.

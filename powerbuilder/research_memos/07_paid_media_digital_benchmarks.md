# Paid Media: Digital Benchmarks for Civic Engagement Campaigns

**Source:** Powerbuilder curated corpus, distilled from Analyst Institute brand-lift studies, Pew Research digital news consumption reports, AdImpact civic-engagement spend trackers, and APIAVote/AAPI Data community media surveys.
**Date:** 2025-10-02
**Document type:** paid media benchmarks
**Topics:** paid media, digital, cpm, ctr, persuasion, gotv, budget estimation, meta, youtube, ctv, programmatic

## How to read this document

These are nonpartisan civic-engagement benchmarks (voter education, registration, turnout, issue awareness for 501(c)(3) and 501(c)(4) programs). They are not commercial-advertising benchmarks (a c3-friendly civic ad on YouTube clears at a different CPM than a DTC brand spot). When the budget agent is asked "what does $X buy me," it should reach for these numbers, not generic eMarketer commercial averages.

All figures are 2024 cycle, US, English-language unless noted. In-language (Spanish, Vietnamese, Korean, Tagalog, Mandarin, Hindi) inventory typically clears 15 to 30 percent under the English CPMs in the same DMA because demand is thinner.

## CPM ranges by channel (civic, US, 2024)

- **Meta (Facebook + Instagram, feed + reels):** $7 to $14 CPM for broad civic awareness, $14 to $28 CPM for narrowly geo-targeted GOTV (county or precinct cluster) in competitive states. Reels inventory runs 20 to 40 percent under feed.
- **YouTube (in-stream skippable, TrueView):** $9 to $18 CPM for civic education creative. Bumper ads (6 second non-skippable): $18 to $32 CPM. Connected-TV YouTube clears higher: $28 to $45 CPM.
- **CTV (Hulu, Roku, Samsung Ads, Pluto, programmatic via The Trade Desk or DV360):** $35 to $65 CPM for targeted civic-engagement buys. The premium reflects guaranteed completion and lean-back attention. Most c3 budgets cannot sustain CTV at scale and use it for the last two weeks of GOTV only.
- **Programmatic display (DV360, TTD, StackAdapt):** $2 to $6 CPM. High reach, low attention. Use for awareness and retargeting, not persuasion.
- **TikTok (in-feed video):** $8 to $16 CPM for organic-feeling civic creative. Demands native creative, not repurposed Meta video. Skews under-30.
- **Snap (Snap Ads, AR lenses for register-to-vote pushes):** $6 to $12 CPM. Best for under-25 registration drives, not persuasion.
- **Spotify and Pandora (audio):** $14 to $22 CPM for 30-second civic spots. Strong for Spanish-language inventory in Sun Belt DMAs.
- **Connected radio and podcast host-reads:** $25 to $50 CPM for endorsed reads on civic-aligned shows. Quality of the host fit matters more than CPM.

## CTR and engagement benchmarks

- **Meta feed video, civic creative:** 0.8 to 1.4 percent CTR is healthy. Below 0.6 percent, kill the creative. Above 1.6 percent typically means the audience is too narrow and frequency will spike.
- **YouTube TrueView view-through rate (VTR):** 35 to 50 percent for skippable civic creative under 30 seconds. Bumper completion rate is 95+ percent by definition (non-skippable).
- **Display click-through:** 0.05 to 0.12 percent. Treat as awareness, not persuasion.
- **TikTok in-feed:** 1.0 to 2.5 percent CTR if the creative reads as native. Half that if it looks like a TV spot.
- **Email (organic, not paid, but tracked alongside):** 18 to 28 percent open rate, 1.5 to 3.5 percent CTR for civic education content.

## Persuasion CPM (cost per percentage-point lift)

This is the number the budget agent should surface when asked "is this worth it." It comes from Analyst Institute and partner randomized-control studies, 2018 to 2024 cycles, civic-engagement context.

- **Meta video, persuasion creative, 4+ frequency:** $35,000 to $70,000 per percentage point of attitudinal shift in the targeted universe.
- **YouTube TrueView, 30-second civic creative:** $40,000 to $90,000 per point.
- **CTV, 15- or 30-second:** $80,000 to $160,000 per point. Higher cost per point, but the lift sticks longer (3 to 6 weeks post-flight versus 1 to 2 weeks for social).
- **Direct mail (for comparison, not digital):** $90,000 to $180,000 per point in suburban universes, much higher in dense urban.
- **Door knock (for comparison):** $25,000 to $55,000 per point in compact universes. Cheapest per point, capped by walkable density.

The order to think about it: doors first where geography allows, social and YouTube next, CTV only when the budget supports it and the universe is large enough to absorb the CPM.

## Frequency caps and creative rotation

- **Social feed (Meta, TikTok):** cap at 3 per week, 8 per flight. Above that, negative sentiment and cost per result both rise sharply.
- **YouTube:** cap at 4 per week, 12 per flight. Skippable creative tolerates higher frequency than feed.
- **CTV:** cap at 5 per week. Frequency above 6 to 7 reads as "this campaign is desperate" in audience research.
- **Display retargeting:** cap at 10 per week, 25 per flight. Different rules because the audience already converted once.
- **Creative rotation:** swap the lead creative every 10 to 14 days. Civic creative wears out faster than commercial because the audience does not opt in to the topic the way they do for brands.

## Channel mix recommendations by budget size

For a Gwinnett-scale county GOTV program targeting Latinx 18 to 35 (universe ~5,000), a useful starting allocation:

- **Under $25K total:** 70 percent Meta (feed + reels, Spanish + English creative), 20 percent SMS, 10 percent door literature print. No CTV, no YouTube. Doors do the persuasion work.
- **$25K to $75K:** 50 percent Meta, 20 percent YouTube, 15 percent SMS, 10 percent Spotify Spanish-language audio, 5 percent print. Add YouTube once Meta saturates (typically 4 weeks of consistent flight).
- **$75K to $200K:** 35 percent Meta, 25 percent YouTube, 20 percent CTV (last 2 weeks), 10 percent SMS, 5 percent audio, 5 percent print.
- **$200K+:** Same mix as above, plus partnership host-reads on civic-aligned podcasts and bilingual community radio in the DMA.

## What does not work in civic paid media

- Repurposed commercial creative. A 30-second TV spot cut down for Meta does not perform.
- English-only creative in majority-bilingual DMAs. The CPM looks fine, the lift is half.
- Programmatic display as a persuasion channel. It is awareness only.
- "Boost post" on the page. Wastes inventory; always use Ads Manager with a defined audience and a clear objective.
- Geo-targeting at the precinct level inside Meta (the geo-fence rounds to roughly 1 mile, so precinct shapes get smeared). For sub-county targeting, use Meta lookalikes off a registered-voter file or a partner CRM seed list.

## Measurement that survives audit (c3-friendly)

- **Brand lift studies (Meta, YouTube, CTV):** ask attitudinal questions about civic engagement, not candidate or party preference. The platform-native lift study tools support this.
- **Pre/post survey (panel-based):** 1,500-respondent panel, baseline before flight, follow-up 1 to 2 weeks after flight ends. Cost: $15K to $30K per wave.
- **Match-back to voter file:** for GOTV, match the targeted ad audience to the voter file post-election and compare turnout to a hold-out. Requires partner with file access.
- **Avoid:** vanity metrics (impressions, reach) reported without lift context. They are inputs, not outcomes.
